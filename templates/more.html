Motivation

The stakes in urban development have always been high and the associated complexity has consistently increased. The study of cities and their influence across borders has been around for some time, increasingly making reference to issues relating to geography, sociology, economics and politics. Initially studied by Peter Hall, two authors have set out two different research paths: Saskia Sassen focuses on the importance of advanced producer service firms while John Friedman stresses the new international division of labour. The study of global cities has recently moved to the analysis of an emergent global city network, work most closely associated with P.J. Taylor.
